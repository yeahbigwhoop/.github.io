Maui County Council — Feb 18, 2026 Feb 2026

The council confirmed 23 board and commission appointments and passed Bill 183 (6-1-2), amending the West Maui Community Plan to set a 30-foot height limit for the Lāhainā Historic Landmark District — a key post-fire rebuilding guardrail. Other highlights: $10M approved to integrate the Department of Water Supply's water systems, $3M for IT and cybersecurity upgrades, and the county accepted a donation of four Compact Rapid Deployable cell towers for fire and public safety use. Bill 17, repealing social host liability provisions, also passed its final reading.

Maui County Council — Feb 6, 2026 Feb 2026

The council opened by honoring Kamehameha Schools Maui football's second consecutive state championship. Bill 15, allowing factory-built housing in the Lāhainā burn zone, passed its final reading with an amendment requiring that the factories themselves be Hawaii-based. A $10M budget amendment for the Kula Agriculture Park and $886K in opioid settlement funds were approved. Affordable housing also featured heavily: Kehalani Apartments received exemptions enabling a May 2026 construction start, and Hale Pilina's grant funding was redistributed to keep that project on track. Bill 2 (Board of Ethics salary reform) and Bill 17 (social host liability repeal) each had their first readings.
